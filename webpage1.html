NAM: Our Country's Never Ending Story

How our archives save our country from becoming a falling nation.

     
Eclipse
       
25 Nov, 2025

Image by NAM


Archive. Though many people pronounced it as "achive". If you're not familiar with records and information management topics, you would probably not even know what it is.

The National Archives of Malaysia (Arkib Negara Malaysia) plays a vital role in preserving the nation’s documentary heritage and supporting Malaysia’s development in various aspects. Established to collect, preserve, and provide access to government and historical records, the National Archives serves as an important institution that safeguards the country’s memory while contributing to governance, education, culture, and national identity.

I have visited the National Archives of Malaysia once, and it amazes me how complicated and precise in managing archives material. One of the most significant contributions of the National Archives of Malaysia is the preservation of historical records. These records include government documents, treaties, photographs, maps, audio-visual materials, and private collections of national importance. As a researcher and an interviewer, I was given a chance to witness how different records got preserved. For paper materials with slight damage, they are usually repaired through traditional repair. Every step was done with full concentration and total carefullness, of course actually because since any slight mistake could cause the losing od vital information from the past.

In addition, the National Archives of Malaysia supports research and education. Students, academics, historians, and researchers rely on archival materials to study Malaysia’s history, independence movement, governance, and cultural development. The availability of authentic primary sources allows researchers to produce accurate and credible studies, strengthening historical understanding and academic scholarship. Educational programs, exhibitions, and publications organised by the Archives also help raise public awareness about Malaysia’s past.

There are many departments in the National Archives of Malaysia that each has its function, but all functions with the same objective, which is to preserve the national archives materials. Some departments are open to be visited by researchers, some aren't. This video from a group of students from Universiti Teknologi Mara (UiTM) did research on this particular matter.

Video: IMD 313 Group Assignment

Moreover, the Archives plays an important role in supporting government administration. Proper records management ensures that government agencies can retrieve information efficiently, make informed decisions, and comply with legal and administrative requirements. The National Archives provides guidelines, training, and standards for records management across public institutions, which enhances efficiency and accountability in the public sector.

Longlasting memories of our culture

In recent years, the National Archives of Malaysia has also embraced digital initiatives. The digitisation of records and online access platforms has made historical documents more accessible to the public, both locally and internationally. This digital transformation supports information sharing, protects fragile originals, and aligns Malaysia with global archival practices.

In conclusion, the National Archives of Malaysia has significantly helped Malaysia by preserving national heritage, supporting research and education, strengthening national identity, improving government administration, and embracing digital innovation. Its role extends beyond record-keeping, as it actively contributes to the country’s cultural preservation, governance, and long-term development. Through its continuous efforts, the National Archives remains a cornerstone of Malaysia’s historical and institutional foundation.
