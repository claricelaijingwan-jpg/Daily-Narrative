Behind The Scenes of a Diploma Student's Life

It becomes a quiet battle between strength and loneliness.

     
Cherii / Ink & Influence
       
26 Mac, 2025

Image by Internet Source


People often say that student life is the best phase of life. They imagine freedom, fun and independence. What they rarely talk about is the emotional that cost of growing up far away from home. For a diploma student who becomes a ‘budak perantauan’, life is no longer just about attending classes and submitting assignments. It becomes a quiet battle between strength and loneliness.

The first few weeks feel unreal. You arrive with luggage full of clothes and a heart full of mixed emotions. You smile, laugh and tell everyone that you are fine but deep inside, there is fear. Saying goodbye to family feels temporary at first. Well, until the nights become silent and you realise that home is no longer just a few steps away.

Mornings That Feel Empty

Mornings used to feel warm and familiar, where someone or in particular your parent, would ask whether you had eaten. Now, mornings begin with an alarm that feels too loud and too sudden.. You wake up alone, prepare yourself quietly with a blank mind and head to class with the thoughts of your family still lingering. Some days you feel strong enough to handle it. Meanwhile, some days you feel tired before the day even begins..

There are moments when you stand in front of the mirror and wondering when everything started to feel this heavy. You miss home not because you are weak, but because you craves for the warmness of home.

Learning to Be Independent Comes with Pain

Yes it is true that living away from home teaches independence, but it also teaches loneliness. You learn how to manage money even when it feels like it disappears too quickly. You learn how to cook simple meals, survive on instant food and do your own laundry after long days. More than that, you learn how to sit with your emotions when no one is around.. sighh

There are nights when ‘rindu suasana rumah’ feels overwhelming. You doom scrolling through old photos, replay voice messages and stare at the ceiling trying to stop the tears.. Sometimes you cry quietly so that no one hears. And sometimes you let yourself breakdown infront of the others because holding it in feels worse. These moments are never planned, but they often happen.

Nights That Break You and Build You

Nighttime is when everything becomes too real. After a long day, when the lights are dim and the room is quiet, emotions come rushing in. Stress from assignments mixes with loneliness. Self-doubt creeps in slowly.. You question yourself whether you are strong enough, good enough or even capable enough.

There are the nights when you cry while doing assignments. Your hands continue typing even when tears fall. You tell yourself that you cannot give up and keep on reminding yourself why you left home in the first place. Growth does not come gently. Sometimes it comes through exhaustion and tears.

Becoming Strong

Somewhere along the journey, change begins to happen. You become more patient with yourself and are capable of handling problems on your own. You begin to realise that the person who once felt lost is now surviving each day with quiet strength. However, you still miss home, but you also realise how much you have grown.

Behind the scenes of a diploma student’s life, there is pain, sacrifice and resilience. It is not a journey filled with constant happiness but it is a journey filled with emotions that shape who you are.. One day, when you finally return home, you will realise that this experience changed you for better.
