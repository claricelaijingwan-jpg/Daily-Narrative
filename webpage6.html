Exploring Seoul: A Muslim-Friendly Guide to the Heart of South Korea

Annyeonghaseyo, fellow travelers!

     
Maryam
       
2 Sep, 2025

Image by Internet Source


South Korea has always been at the top of my bucket list. From the catchy K-Pop beats to the breathtaking scenes in my favorite K-Dramas, I’ve always wanted to experience the "Land of the Morning Calm" for myself. However, as a Muslim traveler, I had my concerns: Will I find Halal food? Are there places to pray?

I’m happy to report that Seoul is incredibly welcoming! Here is my personal journey through Seoul and my top recommendations for a worry-free, soul-satisfying trip.

Itaewon

The Global Hub and Halal Food Heaven No Muslim-friendly trip to Seoul is complete without visiting Itaewon. This is the most multicultural district in the city and home to the Seoul Central Masjid, a stunning piece of architecture perched on a hill.

What to do: Visit the Masjid for your prayers and take in the beautiful blue tile work.

The Food: The streets leading up to the mosque are lined with Halal-certified restaurants. I highly recommend trying EID Halal Korean Food or Makan Halal Korean Restaurant for authentic Halal Bibimbap and Bulgogi.

Gyeongbokgung Palace

Stepping Back in Time Walking through the grand gates of Gyeongbokgung Palace felt like walking onto a drama set. To make the experience extra special, I rented a Hanbok (traditional Korean dress).

Travel Tip: If you wear a Hanbok, entry to the palace is completely free! Many rental shops are "Hijab-friendly" and will help you style your headscarf to match your dress.

Image by Korean Websites


Bukchon Hanok Village

Just a short walk from the palace is this beautiful residential village filled with traditional Korean houses (Hanoks). It’s the perfect place for photos, but remember to stay quiet as people actually live here!

Shopping at Myeongdong

For skincare enthusiasts, Myeongdong is paradise. Beyond the face masks and serums, you can find amazing street food. While not all street food is Halal, you can safely enjoy:

Grilled Lobster with cheese, Egg Bread (Gyeran-ppang) and Freshly swirled 32cm ice cream cones

Useful Tips for Muslim Travelers

Download Halal Korea Apps. It helps you find Halal restaurants and scan barcodes in convenience stores to check for prayer times and ingredients.

Image by Korean Website


Seoul is a city where tradition meets the future, and it is more accessible for Muslims than ever before. The kindness of the locals and the effort put into providing Halal options made me feel right at home. Whether you're here for the shopping, the history, or just to find that perfect "K-Drama" aesthetic, Seoul will not disappoint.
