How Students Actually Survive Assignments and Deadlines

This is not a motivational guide. This is the real experience.

     
Shrlyndria
       
01 Nov, 2025

Image by Internet Source


Let us be honest for a moment. Students do not survive assignments because we are organised or disciplined. We survive because we panic efficiently üò≠ Every semester starts with good intentions, but somehow ends the same way, with late nights, stress, and emotional breakdowns that magically disappear after submission.

This is not a motivational guide. This is the real experience.

The Calm Before the Storm üôÇ

When an assignment is first announced, life feels peaceful (am I right haha). You read the instructions, understand what is needed and confidently tell yourself that you will start early this time. You feel proud. You feel mature. You feel like a new person. That feeling usually lasts for a very short time though...

Days pass and you still have not started but you are not worried. You even convince yourself that you still have plenty of time. However, deep inside, you already know how this story ends but denial feels more comfortable for now.

The Procrastination Era üòå

This is the phase where procrastination takes over your entire personality. Suddenly, everything else feels more important than the assignment itself. Your room has never been cleaner, social media becomes more interesting and even doing absolutely nothing feels productive.

But then, you open the assignment file, stare at it for a few seconds, then close it again. You tell yourself that you work better under pressure (even though past experiences clearly prove otherwise LOL). Still, you continue believing the lie.

Image by Internet Source


Group Assignments and Collective Panic üòµ‚Äçüí´

Group assignments however, bring a different kind of chaos. The group chat stays silent for days. Everyone is busy. Everyone assumes someone else is doing something. Then, late at night, one message appears reminding everyone about the deadline. And suddenly, everyone replies at the same time. Messages flood the chat. Confusion starts. Someone asks what they are supposed to do. Someone says they have already finished their part. Someone has become a ghost. Microsoft word and Canva turn into a stressful mess of moving cursors and very last minute edits. Somehow, no matter how chaotic it gets, this situation happens every single semester.

The Legendary Night üåô

The night before submission is when students unlock quite a superpower hahaha. Sleep no longer matters, coffee becomes a necessity and somehow ideas come faster than ever. You started to type quickly and hoping your brain does not shut down halfway through. Canva designs are rushed. Paragraphs are written with tired eyes.

Once again, you promise yourself that this will be the last time you do things ‚Äúlast minute‚Äù (knowing very well that you will forget this promise after submission wompwomp). At this point, you are no longer aiming for perfection. You are aiming for survival.

The Fear After Submission üò≠

Submitting the assignment does not bring instant relief. Instead, anxiety kicks in. You refresh the page to make sure it really went through. Then, you open the file again to check whether you uploaded the correct version. Next, you are going to replay every decision you made while doing the assignment‚Ä¶ Even after everything is done, your heart still feels uneasy. This feeling is something only students truly understand.

Chaos but We Survive üí™

Students do not survive because we do everything right. We survive because we adapt. We procrastinate, panic, struggle but still manage to submit. It is chaotic, stressful and sometimes funny when we look back at it.

Surviving assignments and deadlines is not about being perfect. It is about making it through, one chaotic submission at a time.
